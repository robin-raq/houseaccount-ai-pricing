Documented modeling approach

How the estimate is made

The data, the features, the model, and — most importantly — how confidence is computed and when the engine admits it's guessing.

Data & model

  • 1,432 sanitized jobs, 18 categories; 411 carry a final price (training signal)
  • Correction model on the previous estimate: gradient-boosted residual on log-price
  • Features: prior estimate + band, ZIP region, subtype, urgency, seasonality, and LLM-extracted scope (complexity, labor hours, area, material tier)
  • Normalized split-conformal intervals — calibrated, distribution-free

How confidence is calculated

Confidence comes from the conformal interval width relative to the estimate, scaled by the previous model's band so harder jobs read as less certain. Mapped to [0,1] and clamped.

Out-of-distribution → forced < 0.5 (never rejected)

  • Midpoint > $5,000
  • Interval wider than 3× the median observed range
  • Category outside the 10 production verticals
